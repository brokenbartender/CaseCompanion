Exhibit Z — Combined Victim Impact Summary
Victims: Cody Elis McKenzie & Shelby Herron
Incident Date: August 20, 2025
Location: Liberty Bar / LBP Entertainment Group LLC
Purpose:
This exhibit provides a consolidated summary of the physical, emotional, and financial impact experienced by both victims, Cody McKenzie and Shelby Herron, as a result of the assault on August 20, 2025. It is intended to give prosecutors a clear, unified understanding of the harm caused to both individuals.
------------------------------------------------------------
1. IMPACT ON SHELBY HERRON (PRIMARY FEMALE VICTIM)
------------------------------------------------------------
Physical Impact:
• Shelby was the initial target of the aggressor’s violence.
• She was struck in the face and had a drink thrown on her.
• She experienced immediate pain, shock, and physical disorientation.
Emotional Impact:
• Shelby displayed visible fear and distress throughout the incident.
• She sought safety, moved away from the aggressor, and secured a witness.
• The assault caused lasting anxiety, hypervigilance, and emotional disruption.
Behavioral Indicators of Trauma:
• Quiet, shaken demeanor in video evidence.
• Avoidance of the aggressor and reliance on bystanders for safety.
• Difficulty processing the sudden escalation of violence.
------------------------------------------------------------
2. IMPACT ON CODY E. MCKENZIE (MALE VICTIM / RESTRAINER)
------------------------------------------------------------
Physical Impact:
• Cody sustained shoulder, torso, and lower‑body strain from restraining the aggressor.
• He was kicked forcefully while performing a protective restraint.
• He experienced pain, reduced mobility, and ongoing physical symptoms.
Emotional Impact:
• Cody experienced fear, stress, and adrenaline‑driven exhaustion during the incident.
• He was placed in a position of danger while attempting to protect Shelby.
• The aggressor’s continued escalation, even after standing beside an exit, increased Cody’s sense of threat.
Financial Impact:
• Cody’s injuries contributed to missed work, reduced hours, and medical expenses.
• The aftermath included retaliation, wage withholding, and eventual termination.
• These consequences created significant financial instability.
------------------------------------------------------------
3. SHARED IMPACT ON BOTH VICTIMS
------------------------------------------------------------
• Both victims were subjected to an unprovoked, escalating act of violence.
• Both experienced fear, uncertainty, and emotional distress during and after the incident.
• Both relied on each other and bystanders for safety.
• Both were forced into a defensive posture due to the aggressor’s continued hostility.
• The incident disrupted their sense of safety in a public setting.
------------------------------------------------------------
4. AGGRESSOR BEHAVIOR CONTRIBUTING TO HARM
------------------------------------------------------------
• The aggressor admitted to battery (“I threw my drink on her”).
• He never denied punching Shelby.
• He violently resisted restraint and kicked Cody.
• He refused to retreat despite standing beside an exit.
• His behavior created a prolonged threat to both victims.
------------------------------------------------------------
5. OVERALL IMPACT SUMMARY
------------------------------------------------------------
The assault on August 20, 2025 caused significant and lasting harm to both victims. Shelby suffered direct physical and emotional trauma as the primary target of the aggressor’s violence. Cody suffered physical injury, emotional strain, and substantial financial consequences while attempting to protect her and prevent further harm. The aggressor’s escalating behavior, refusal to retreat, and continued aggression intensified the impact on both individuals.
This combined summary reflects the totality of harm experienced by both victims and supports the charges and evidence presented in this case.
